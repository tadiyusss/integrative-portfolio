Bike to Bytes: My Journey into Tech and Cybersecurity

From Pedals to Programs: How a Passion for Exploration Led Me to Cybersecurity and Tech

Childhood

I was born in Muntinlupa, where my childhood was filled with fun and excitement.1 I loved spending time outdoors, playing with my friends, and exploring the neighborhood.2 My early years were filled with laughter and adventure as I engaged in various activities.3 I have one brother, and like most siblings, we had our fair share of fights and playful moments.4 Despite our differences, we always found ways to bond and have fun together.5 My favorite hobbies included playing outside, watching cartoons, biking, and participating in sports.6

Highschool

High school was a mix of good and bad experiences for me.1 I was not particularly focused on studying during those years, but I am grateful for the lessons I learned before entering college.2 One of the highlights of my high school years was being a member of the school's football team.3 It was an exciting experience that allowed me to meet new people during tournaments and develop teamwork skills.4 While I did not have a part-time job during my junior high school years, I started creating simple websites for clients when I reached senior high school.5 However, I faced some struggles during this time.6 I was quite stubborn and often skipped school to play billiards, which affected my academic performance.7

College

Currently, I am studying at the University of the East, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology.1 I chose this field because of my deep interest in computer security and programming.2 The transition from senior high school to college was challenging since I had to adjust to a completely new environment without knowing anyone at the university.3 However, I gradually adapted and found my place.4 I am actively involved in college organizations and currently serve as the Division Head of Cybersecurity in the CCSS Research and Development team.5 Balancing studies and social life is essential for me, and I always prioritize my academics to avoid repeating subjects, which would require extra time and money.6
